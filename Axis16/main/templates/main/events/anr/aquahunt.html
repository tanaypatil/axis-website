Its the Marvel's cinematic universe. The theme is based around Thor, a Morse God and one of the several avengers.The world of Asgard is under attack and is getting destroyed by Loki's army. Thor is helpless at this moment as he is at Earth without his hammer.In this universe, the path between Earth and Asgard is a sea with gushing waters. Loki's army produces uncanny changes and turbulence in the water around the world of Asgard too.Your job is to design a ship to rescue the people from Asgard and carry them to a safer place i.e. Earth through the challenging sea-path. Another feat that your ship has to accomplish is to bring back Thor's hammer which he left at Asgard.

Will you be able to rescue the citizens of Asgard?

Will you be able to unite Thor to his Hammer before Loki destroys everything?

  1. The basic task of the bot in the ‘First Mission’ of the two will be to navigate safely through the arena and
    place the objects on strategic locations and points awarded will be proportional to the difficulty and
    reach of the checkpoints and the blocks placed.


  2. The objects comprises of cubical wooden blocks and a T-shaped wooden block. The cubical block correspond to
    the Asgard people stuck at the time of destruction of their world. The T-shaped block correspond
    to Thor’s Hammer. Points awarded will differ for both objects.


  3. An idea about the strategic places can be found in the Arena Specifications.


  4. The task focuses more on the bot’s grabbing efficiency and the navigator’s skill.


  5. The Thor’s Hammer (T-shaped block) can only be grabbed after rescuing minimum five Asgard citizens (i.e. cubical blocks).


  6. If the objects (people) are dropped midway in water, they can be rescued from the raging sea path
    in a specified time period (Refer Rules) else they will be considered dead and cannot be
    rescued and points will be deducted for the same.


  7. More than one object can be carried simultaneously by the bot to utilize the time properly.


  8. Sea’s turbulence (minor) might be simulated which correspond to the Loki's army around the Asgard
    and hence bot must be well designed to avoid toppling as hand touches will cause deduction in points.


  9. Details and Tasks for the ‘Second Mission’ will be declared during the event although no modifications
    in the bot will be needed for completing them.

  • The points system described below are for the ‘First Round’ only.
  • The points for the Second Round will be disclosed at the time of ‘Second Round’ itself, although no
    modifications in the design of bots is required for the ‘Second Round’.
  • Each team will be given 10 minutes to complete the tasks/event.
    For testing of their bots, 2 extra minutes will be given before the start of tasks/event.
  • The points are decided according to placement of the blocks on the safety islands ranging from 10 to 30.
  • Every time, the bot passes the turbulence region, extra 5 points will be awarded.
  • If the team drops any block into the water, 5 points will be deducted from their total score.
  • There is a limitation in the number of hand-touches allowed during the tasks/event.
    The first hand touch is allowed with no change in the total score.
    However, each hand touch after the first, will lead to deduction of 10 points from the total score.
  • Grabbing and placing the T-shaped block ( Thor’s Hammer) successfully on any of the safety islands will be awarded with extra 100 points.
  • Innovation in the design of bots will be awarded with 50 points.
  • The ‘Second Round’ will be disclosed at the time of the event.
  • Event Managers’ decision at the time of event will be Final and Binding.

  • The robot should fit in a box of 30 x 30 x 30 cm in its most compact form.
  • A Maximum of 5 people can be there in a group.
  • The robot has to be necessarily controlled by some wired/wireless remote controlled mechanism at all stages of the event.
  • The controlling wires should not be held taut at any point of the event.
  • Wireless bot will be awarded extra points in preliminary round i.e. the ‘First Mission’.
  • Use of Lego kits or readymade mechanisms is strictly prohibited. Violation of this clause will lead to disqualification of the robot. However, readymade gear and shafts may be used.
  • The potential difference between any two points on the bot should not exceed 12V.
  • A 12V power supply will be provided by us.
  • Damage to the arena by the bot may result in disqualification.
  • Event managers’ decision will be final and binding.
