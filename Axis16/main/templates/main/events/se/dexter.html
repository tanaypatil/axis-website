"Talent hits a target no one else can hit; Genius hits a target no one else can see.”

Do you think you possess the ability to hit the bull’s eye of invisible targets? Can you find answers where others can’t see any? We bring to you an event that will push you to your limits and make you explore the genius residing inside. DEXTER is not just an aptitude test. It is a journey that puts your intellectual as well as interactive skills to test. Taking forward the huge success from last year, we present to you an opportunity to identify your skills. We will encourage and help you to enhance your endowments. Through DEXTER, we aim to not just find an intelligent kid, but to find and help groom a complete individual equipped with all the tools required to excel in this competitive world. Students will learn to open up their minds, think out of the box, and gain a whole new interactive experience. Also, a healthy competitive spirit will be inculcated among the participants. AXIS aims to slightly nudge the dexterity that resides in these minds and to provide a platform for them to showcase their competence, thus taking a step towards innovation and excellence.

  1. Registration fees -
    Minimal registration fees of Rupees 30 will be charged.

  2. Test dates -
    Phase I - 4 July – 15 July 2016
    (The schools can select any date from 4 July-15 July 2016 based on their
    convenience, provided the date has not been booked by other schools in the city.
    The exam will be conducted in a span of 1 hour in the school timings or otherwise)
    Phase II - September 23-24 2016 in Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology, Nagpur

  3. Structure of Question Paper -
    15 questions, both subjective and objective testing data interpretation, logic deduction, and mental ability

  1. Round 1:-
    Purely merit based. After analyzing all the papers, cutoff will be decided “RELATIVELY” based on the overall
    performance. There is no “ABSOLUTE” cutoff pre-decided. Keeping in mind the fact that a 12th grade child
    may have an edge over an 8th class child, cutoff for lower classes can be assumed to be lower than the
    higher classes, so as not give any advantage of age/experience to higher class students.

  2. Round 2:-
    Round 2 remains a surprise for the children with many skills to be judged on, thus, every individual is carefully
    scrutinized according to the skill which the round tests. A marking system is devised and based on the individual
    performance; he/she is allotted marks accordingly. Toppers are then decided on their total score of
    Round 2 (Round 1 marks don’t play any role here).
