Breathe life into any one of the topics described in the problem statements by making a 3D, 2D or Stick Figure Animation video, jazz it up with multifarious characters and animated effects to exemplify your unique perception of the topic, and showcase your originality and ingenuity to enthral the judges and sweep them off their feet!

  • TIME TRAVEL, that is movement between different points in time, is a fascinating concept that held human fancy since time immemorial. One of mankind’s wildest fantasies, it has been a mind-boggling thing. And ironically enough, it’s probably impossible!

  • Ever wondered how a basket of eggs feel, as you take them out of the boiling pan one-by-one, peel one of their friends as the others watch in sheer agony, and proceed to brutally mutilate each by slicing your careless knife through their warm bodies? What if inanimate objects could talk and express their feelings? The second problem statement, REACTIONS OF THINGS, invites you to get your creative juices flowing and come up with something hilarious (or maybe downright atrocious), and showcase to the world that inanimate objects do have feelings!

Winners are judged by jury of invited industry professionals. All jury decisions are final and binding.

Any overtly offensive content as judged by the jury stands disqualified.

Judging will include following criteria :

  1. Originality/creativity
    Is this entry a new idea? Or have we seen it dozens of times before?
  2. Theme match
    How well does this submission relate to the theme?
  3. Technical difficulty
    How technically challenging is the implementation?
  4. Quality
    How well is it executed? Does it have especially attractive original graphics?
  5. Good submissions do not need to score highly on all criteria, but should aim to score high on several of the criteria,
    and not too low on any.

  1. What is the difference in animation 2D and 3D?
    The most obvious difference between the two genres of animation is the three dimensional characteristics
    or the appearance of depth. 2D animation is created by drawing almost every frame of the animated
    movie and is the base of 3D animation. 3D animation is created by changing the poses and the
    placement of already created 3D models. It has more depth and is more realistic, it is a series of
    images/ objects created using computers or any other digital media.
