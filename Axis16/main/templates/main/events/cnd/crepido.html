Bridge the gap between galaxies! Crepido puts your mechanical proficiency against gravity. Construct solid, secure bridges from flimsy popsicles sticks and simple glue. Show off your talent, creativity and dexterity. Can your bridge stand the test of gravity as it takes on unimaginable loads? Showcase the marvel in your perception; show the world the build within. Bring a robust and captivating one and remember…Triangles are strong!

Design a truss bridge using popsicle sticks (icecream sticks) satisfying the stated constraints.
Materials to be used

  • Use popsicle sticks.
  • Maximum number of popsicle sticks to be used: 500
  • Only fevicol can be used as an adhesive, use of other adhesives may lead to disqualifications.
  • Use of threads is not allowed.
Overall bridge dimensions
  • Length = not more than 70 cm, not less than 60cm
  • Width = 10-12 cm (approximate length of one stick)
  • Height (max.) = Refer the sample image
  • Cross section of supporting columns (max) = 8x8 cm
Weight of the bridge
  • The weight of the bridge should not exceed the weight of 500 popsicle sticks
Testing the bridge


  • Teams must submit bridge 1 hour before
    the competition for inspection.
  • Inspection will test that the construction rules were
    followed and will record the weight of the bridge.
  • The bridges will then be subjected to loading
    through a crusher canyon. The teams will have
    the freedom to choose the loading points
    (symmetric). Nothing will be provided to keep
    the base of the bridge in place during loading.
  • Once the bridge is in position, the loading tray
    will be attached. The load will hang below the bridge, supported by two bars which rest on the deck of the bridge.
  • Bridges will then be subjected to loading.
Team size
  • 1–4 students per team.

First the structure will be reviewed to check if it violates any rules mentioned above. The scoring of the structure will be based on performance as well as aesthetics.

Performance

  • The greatest load prior to failure will be the bridge’s load capacity.
  • The deflection of the bridge at yielding will be noted. If the deflection increases more than 1 cm,
    then load at the deflection will be taken as bridge’s capacity.
  • Width of deck should not be greater than 1.5 cm.
  • Truss should be on the upper side...
  • The efficiency of the bridge will be calculated.
    Efficiency = Load Capacity of the bridge /Self Weight – Points deducted due to discrepancie

A rise in demand of coal, oil and other minerals was envisaged by the government of a thriving state. They, after days of discussion opted for the use of railroads to transport the minerals and oils. It was favoured as it would allow to transport greater amount of goods in a less time. Moreover, it avoided roadway congestion. A government contractor was assigned a project to lay down railway tracks to facilitate the transport of minerals and oils to the state. But the railway lines could not be connected as they encountered a river. Engineers decided to connect the railway lines through a truss bridge over the river which would have the strength to bear a heavy freight locomotive. Now, imagine yourself in their position and construct a truss bridge using popsicle sticks. Use your inventiveness to make a sturdy and aesthetically pleasing bridge.
