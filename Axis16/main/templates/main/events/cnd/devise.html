A workshop to introduce Bamboo as a building material to the students.
Idea: A 3-day workshop organized at VNIT, Nagpur.
When: 10, 11, 12 September, 2016
For whom: Students of Architecture
Venue: VNIT, Nagpur
Timings: 8.00 am to 6.00 pm every day



Structure:
The workshop is primarily hands-on experience for the students and young professionals.
This will expose the participants to the nature of bamboo, get familiar with the material, and make
them experience its behavior when used in different conditions in a building-structure.
A small group of say about 30 participants(2nd and 3rd year) and 20 participants (first year) will
work with the bamboo to understand the material and its applications.
This will be a mix of some class-room sharing of ideas along with exploration and experimentation
with bamboo that will happen, hands on. Typically a day will have a 1 class-room session and 2 work-sessions.

Day 1
Session 1: ‘Breaking the Ice’; Introduction to the workshop, an introduction to the group and getting started.
Session 2: Getting to know Bamboo – exploring some basic working-methods, tools and techniques of working the
material. This will include cutting, splitting, splicing, drilling etc.
The first day, all years, i.e., first, second and third year will work together.
At the end of session 2, a one hour session would be held to design and explore with bamboo.
These designed units would be made by the first year during the next two days in a parallel session.

Day 2
From day 2, the second and third year would work on building a 1:1 scale model of the built form.
Session 1: Understanding of the entire structure, layouting of the entire structure on site with the help of
few volunteers. The division of the group in 5 parts. Each will make 5 components of the structure.
Session 2: Preparation of the structural components of the built form in 5 groups( 6 people each).

Day 3:
Session 1: The components put together by the group along with necessary added components, to make a kit that
will help group put a structure together. It will be an exercise in assembly of structural members
to make a complete built-form. In doing so the group will understand and be able to relate with
issues of junctions, details etc.
Session 2: The assembly of the structure to continue.
Concluding session: Share experiences from participants. Feed-back from the participants etc.
